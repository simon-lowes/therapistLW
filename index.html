About Me

Before starting my practice, I studied integrative counselling for four years in Peterborough and have since completed a BA Hons in Counselling & Psychotherapy as well as becoming a qualified clinical supervisor.

I have worked within the NHS, MIND, and two local counselling charities. I currently work with the 8:56 Foundation in King's Lynn and offer fully funded counselling for men.

I am a registered member of The British Association of Counsellors and Psychotherapists (BACP), so have regular supervision meetings and am committed to continual professional development.

I have additional training in mental health first aid, women's health, chronic illness, couples therapy, and applied suicide intervention skills training.

I am an integrative counsellor, through my training in different approaches, so have flexibility in how we work together. Through the therapeutic relationship, we will find a way that works best for you and may explore techniques and tools to help you manage or navigate difficult situations. I am unable to offer advice, as I work with the client's agenda, but I will offer different perspectives and ask open questions to prompt reflection ‚Äì all within a confidential and safe space.

Privacy Policy

The purpose of this statement is to inform you about the personal information I collect and hold, why I collect this data, how long it is stored for and your rights over your personal data.

I am committed to complying with the terms of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Data Protection Act 2018, and to the responsible and secure use of your data. I have a legitimate basis to collect data for the purpose of delivering counselling services.

I am registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

Information About You

When you enquire about counselling, I ask for contact details and relevant personal information that is needed to answer your enquiries and to keep you informed.

If you choose to proceed with counselling, I will keep an adequate and relevant record that is limited to personal details, session notes, attendances, payments, and other relevant information gathered in the course of this provision.

Once counselling ends, all data in your file is stored securely for 2 years, and then destroyed.

Use of This Information

Your data will be used to provide you with my services and to meet the requirements of my regulatory body, the British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy (BACP), and my insurer.

I will not share your details with any other person or organisation without your knowledge and permission unless there is a legal or ethical requirement as stated in the counselling contract.

Your information will not be sold or shared with third parties for marketing purposes.

Your Rights

If you have any concerns about my use of your data, please contact me directly. If for any reason I cannot resolve the issue, you can choose to contact the ICO directly at ico.org.uk.
